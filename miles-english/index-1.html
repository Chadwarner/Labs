Every single answer uses this formula. Start by directly answering the question (your point). Drop in a short quote. Explain what the specific word or phrase actually means — not just restating it, but analysing what the writer is doing. Then link back to the theme or the exam question.

  • You don't need every scene — just know the 6 key story beats per text
  • Higher marks come from talking about context: Jacobean audiences, Victorian poverty, the author's intentions
  • The word "suggests" is your best friend — it shows analysis, not summary
  • Don't retell the story. Answer the question. One good paragraph beats three weak ones.
