Every single answer uses this formula. Start by directly answering the question. Drop in a short quote. Explain what the specific word or phrase actually means — analyse what the writer is doing, not just what they're saying. Then link back to the theme.

  • You don't need every scene — know the 6 key beats per text
  • Higher marks come from context: Jacobean beliefs, Victorian poverty, author's intentions
  • "Suggests" is your best word — it signals analysis, not summary
  • Don't retell the story. One sharp paragraph beats three weak ones.
